Welcome to the Testing skills map repo!

Create the map of your own testing skills using a template and guidelines, and view maps created by others

Some guidelines

To identify the skills, try the following flow:

Step 1: Identify problem-solving situations

Think of a situation when you tried to solve a problem while doing some test activity and identify the following steps:

  1. the difficulty you felt
  2. the location of the problem
  3. the possible solutions you found
  4. how you experimented with and tested solutions
  5. the conclusion regarding the acceptance or rejection of the suggested solution/s

Taking notes while thinking about each of the steps can help in the analysis of the situation

These steps are presented in "How we think" as a skeleton for the reflection process

Step 2: Identify the skills you used

For each of the steps in the reflection process, think about what skills you used. Here are some heuristics which can help you to identify the skills and not mix them with something else:

  • A table in the BBST Instructors' course differentiating between different elements in the knowledge dimension, and their different levels in the cognitive process dimension
    Knowledge Dimension Cognitive process dimension
    Remember Understand Apply Analyze Evaluate Create
    Facts

    Procedures

Step 3: List them under categories in your map

Step 4: Repeat

Remember, what's more important than the resulting map is the reflection process you practice with each time you go through the flow. With time and deliberate practice, the map will become more relevant and you will notice interesting patterns about your own skills evolution.

Step 5: Share your map in the collective repo

You can share your own map in an anonymous way and you can check out other maps to get some inspiration
